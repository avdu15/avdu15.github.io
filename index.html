Strawberry Cheesecake Cookies

Prep time: 50 minutes
Bake time: 15 minutes

Looking for something perfect for picnics, parties, or even as a special treat for yourself? These cookies are a great way to enjoy the classic flavors of strawberry cheesecake in a handheld, easy-to-eat form. They are super easy to make and come together in under an hour from start to finish which is unbelievable! They are perfect for any occasion from birthdays to holidays, these are a true crowd-pleaser!
Let's get started!!

1. Into a medium mixing bowl, add the softened cream cheese and powdered sugar, and mix with an electric hand mixer until well combined and smooth.
2. Line a small baking sheet with parchment paper and scoop 12 teaspoon of the cream cheese mixture on it. Freeze until harden for about 40+ minutes.
3. Once the cream cheese filling is frozen, keep it in the freezer and start making the cookie dough.
4. Into a big mixing bowl, add the room temperature butter, sugar, brown sugar, kosher salt, and vanilla extract, and cream it with an electric hand mixer for 2-3 minutes or until it's creamy and light in colour.
5. Add the eggs, and mix until combined and creamy.
6. Then, add the flour, and baking soda, and mix with the hand mixer until almost combined.
7. Add the chopped strawberries and mix until just combined and there are no lumps of flour visible. Do not overmix.
8. Use a large cookie scooper to scoop out the cookies or scoop the cookies with a regular tablespoon, 2 tablespoons at a time. Place them on a parchment paper-lined baking sheet.
9. Using your thumb or a teaspoon, make indents on each cookie.
10. Remove the frozen cream cheese from the freezer and place each cream cheese piece in the indent you make in each cookie dough ball.
11. Form the dough into a ball using your clean hands and bake in a 180c (350f) preheated oven for 12-14 minutes or until the edges of the cookies are light golden brown. Do not overbake them, we want to keep these cookies soft.
12. Let the cookies cool for 5 minutes, and then transfer them to a wire rack to cool completely.
